Reflow Oven Controller

Using a regular oven to reflow PCBs

Engineering lab course | Feburary, 2018

A reflow oven must follow a certain temperature curve to properly bake PCBs. The goal of this project is to make a control unit to control the temperature of the oven to follow the reflow temperature curve. 

Figure 1: Completed Oven Controller

We used a solid-state relay to turn the oven on and off for temperature control. We used a thermocouple to detect the temperature in the oven and graphed it on top of the reflow temperature profile.

Figure 2: The reflow oven temperature graph. Current temperature is displayed in green. The reflow temperature profile displayed behind the current temperature

As you can see, the temperature is not following the profile very well. This is because heating/cooling follows an exponential curve, making them difficult to control. I started recording data about how fast the temperature changes versus how high the temperature is. I made a few hypotheses, tested them and implemented my findings to the control logic. The result is a much more accurate oven controller. The improved controller shown in figure 3 is quite obviously superior to the controller shown in figure 2.

Figure 3: Oven Controller improved with my tests and findings

The oven controller worked wonderfully and successfully churned out the surface-mount microcontroller shown in figure 4.


Figure 4: EFM8 surface mount microcontroller
